CombinaTron

a computational design experiment, currently focussing on colors, in 2D user interfaces

by Karan Dudeja

Why

When designing, iterations always benefit decision making. The designer often makes iterations based on a lot of intuitive processes that depend on their exposure, experience, knowledge and interest levels in the project. Since the output of a design activity is subjective, it may not align with the aesthetic preferences of everyone involved in the project. More over, the designer being human can reach a level of saturation, which is seldom conveyed explicitly as it can hurt their ego. At that point of saturation, asking the designer for another iteration, regardless of how sweetly one asks, can give rise to 'resistance'. This resistance emerges due to realisation of the effort required to create more and different from what has already been proposed. Additionally the cognizance of, a paradoxical effect that higher number of options/iterations have on this choice making process i.e. of making it more difficult, can support that internal resistance.

However, computers offer no resistance(of human-type yet) when at work. Therefore, would it not be pleasant if the machine was able to provide you with all possible iterations(or seen as combinations) of the variables in your design task?

In such a case, a designer would have to offer the right set of variables and a few other parameters as constraints, to the machine. From the options generated, the designer or the decision maker will have to only choose their most preferred option. Could this be a new form of teamwork between the man-machine duo?

The motivation for creating this experimental tool comes from a personal experience of facing saturation when designing solo and the 'resistance' while collaborating. There were questions; What would it be like if all the iterations were available, to choose the "best" option from? How will this impact the time spent and where in the process? Will a tool that can create iterations, allow me to (quickly or not) explore more creative possibilities?

What

CombinaTron is a computational design experiment generates combinations of colors for a Graphical User Interface(GUI). The combinations are created based on the GUI Elements and Colors selected by the user(designer). In mobile/desktop screens, the GUIs consists of the Background and elements that make up the Foreground.

The Foreground in a GUI generally has Text, Images, Graphical Elements(horizontal dividers, checkboxes, buttons, icons, sliders) etc. Some of these GUI elements are interactive while others are not. Although in terms of designing the GUI of a page, one needs to account for the types of elements, their location, size and color. All these properties(scale, color, etc.) of each element can be varied.

This experiment is focussing on colors, since a limited number of colors(as too many can be jarring) is a good constraint for computing the possible number of combinations on a 2D GUI.
CombinaTron produces combinations, from the selected GUI elements and colors. The combinations created are rendered as cards. The color-contrast indicators(bottom right) on these cards are as per the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines(WCAG) 2.1. The colors of the background and the text elements(title or body) are considered while calculating the contrast ratio.

Future Scope

- Handle more parameters of color; create gradients, etc.
- Experiment with other parameters of visual design and composition; position, scale, etc.
- Integrate machine learning.
- Create an algorithm that can(or would only) generate "appropriate" iterations.

The project can make one wonder, what is the role of a designer or are they becoming curators in presence of a machine that can handle the clerical tasks. The computer is an incredible machine that can offer a plethora of creative possibilities, the surface of which i/we are trying to scratch.
